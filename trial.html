GLOBAL TERRORISM

Terrorism is a form of violence and intimidation that seeks to achieve political, ideological, or religious objectives through the deliberate targeting of civilians or non-combatants. It involves the use of fear, terror, and violence to instigate fear in a population and influence the decisions and policies of governments or organizations. Examples of terrorist acts include bombings, assassinations, hijackings, and cyberattacks. Notable terrorist groups include Al-Qaeda, responsible for the 9/11 attacks in the United States, ISIS (Islamic State of Iraq and Syria), known for its global extremist activities, and the Irish Republican Army (IRA), which operated in Northern Ireland and sought to end British rule through violence. Combating terrorism requires international cooperation, intelligence-sharing, and efforts to address the root causes that may drive individuals or groups towards extremism.
